The first Saturday in March brought sunshine and about forty volunteers to Goddard Park, where the Parks & Recreation Committee organized a spring cleanup and planting day.

## More Than Maintenance

Committee member Ray Chen arrived at 7 a.m. to set up stations — mulching, trail repair, native plantings along the creek bank. But he said the real purpose was something bigger.

"People think Parks and Rec is about mowing grass," Chen laughed. "It's really about building a place people want to be. And the best way to do that is to build it together."

## The Regulars and the Newcomers

Among the volunteers were a mix of longtime residents and families who'd moved to the township in the last year. Several said they came because they'd seen the event posted at the township building.

"We don't really know our neighbors yet," said one new resident, planting switchgrass plugs along the stream bank. "This seemed like a good way to start."

## Looking Ahead

The committee is planning two more volunteer days this spring, plus a summer concert series in the park pavilion. Details will be posted on the township website and shared here on London Grove Commons.
