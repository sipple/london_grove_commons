Ken Battin has been at the helm of London Grove Township for years, quietly keeping the gears of local government turning — roads, zoning, budgets, and everything in between.

## Behind the Desk

We met Battin at the township building on Rose Hill Road on a gray January morning. He was already mid-coffee and mid-email, toggling between a stormwater management question and a call about a pothole on Newark Road.

"That's the job," he said with a shrug. "One minute you're reviewing a land development plan, the next you're figuring out why a stop sign is leaning."

## What Residents Don't See

Battin says the biggest misconception about township government is that nothing happens between meetings.

"There's an enormous amount of coordination that goes on behind the scenes — with the county, with PennDOT, with developers, with utility companies. The Board of Supervisors sets the direction, but the day-to-day work is constant."

He pointed to recent infrastructure projects as an example: culvert replacements, road resurfacing, and ongoing coordination with the state on Route 41 improvements.

## On Community Engagement

When asked what he'd want residents to know, Battin was direct: "We're accessible. If you have a question or a concern, call the office. That's what we're here for."

He also encouraged people to attend committee meetings — not just the Board of Supervisors sessions, but the Planning Commission, Parks and Recreation, and Environmental Advisory Council.

"That's where the real conversations happen," he said. "Those volunteers put in serious time, and more people should see the work they do."

## Looking Ahead

Battin said his focus remains on maintaining the township's rural character while managing the pressures of growth in Chester County.

"People move here because of what London Grove is. Our job is to protect that while still planning for the future."
